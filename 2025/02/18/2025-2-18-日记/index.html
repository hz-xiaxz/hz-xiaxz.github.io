BdG

By plain test, the order parameter should be only summed over negative energy states or only summed over positive energy states.

I can’t tell why still. And I don’t know if this result is related with particle filling.

It’s quite weird if we think of the finite temperature expression

Δ=∣U∣∑u(r)v∗(r)tanh⁡(E2T)\Delta = \left\vert U \right\vert \sum u(r) v^*(r) \tanh(\frac{E}{2T}) Δ=∣U∣∑u(r)v∗(r)tanh(2TE​)

We know that as T→0T\to 0 T→0,

tanh⁡(xT)→f(x)={−1x<01x>0\tanh (\frac{x}{T}) \to f(x) = \begin{cases} -1 & x < 0 \\ 1 & x > 0 \end{cases} tanh(Tx​)→f(x)={−11​x<0x>0​

I think the derivation from Zhu’s book is correct.

Δ(r,r′)=V(r−r′)∑u(r)v∗(r′)f(−E)\Delta(r,r')=V(r-r')\sum u(r)v^*(r')f(-E) Δ(r,r′)=V(r−r′)∑u(r)v∗(r′)f(−E)

or

Δ(r,r′)=V(r−r′)∑u(r)v∗(r′)f(E)\Delta(r,r')=V(r-r')\sum u(r)v^*(r')f(E) Δ(r,r′)=V(r−r′)∑u(r)v∗(r′)f(E)

Thus he gives

Δ(r,r′)=V(r−r′)2∑u(r)v∗(r′)tanh⁡(E2T)\Delta(r,r')=\frac{V(r-r')}{2} \sum u(r)v^*(r') \tanh (\frac{E}{2T}) Δ(r,r′)=2V(r−r′)​∑u(r)v∗(r′)tanh(2TE​)

(I omit the nnn index 'cause I’mmmmmm lazy!)

This equation make sense, and tells that in the T→0T\to 0T→0 case, f(E)→θ(EF−E)f(E) \to \theta(E_F-E)f(E)→θ(EF​−E). So the first equation is counts all negative energy states, and the second equation counts all positive energy states.

However, I’m a bit puzzled by just looking at the final result.

With this understanding, the result should be affected by the particle filling.

Why set μ=0\mu=0μ=0

If we adapt the equation in Zhu’s book, then the next question is, why the chemical potential is set to zero in the Fermi-Dirac distribution?

Ah I found that in 4.18 , Zhu gives the exact equation. Seems like I should still choose the excited states.

Δ(r,r′)=V(r−r′)∑n(En≥0)un(r)vn∗(r′)\Delta(r,r') = V(r-r') \sum_{n(E_n\ge 0)} u_n(r)v_n^*(r') Δ(r,r′)=V(r−r′)n(En​≥0)∑​un​(r)vn∗​(r′)

Seems like the discussion above are nonsense, hh.

Oh I see. That’s because when we do the Bogoliubov transformation and have the new Hamiltonian

H=Eg+∑nEnγn†γnH = E_g + \sum_n E_n \gamma_n^\dagger \gamma_n H=Eg​+n∑​En​γn†​γn​

in this equation, EnE_nEn​ as excitation energy should be all positive. So the negative energy states in the diagonalization process are not included in the final result.
